**Gitcoin RFP GG23 Retrospective**

**TLDR**

Let's GROW DAO was selected to lead the GG23 Onboarding Program, scheduled for March, just before the GG23 Applications & Donations window. The team designed the initiative around a typical **Dreamer's Journey**, which became the guiding framework for the entire program. This approach focused primarily on supporting **new builders** entering the Gitcoin ecosystem, while also accommodating **established projects** through features like **On-Demand Bookings** for those unable to attend live Office Hours.

Using the Dreamer's Journey as a foundation, we structured everything—from topic selection to the event agenda and timeline—and even produced a dedicated [**animated video**](https://youtu.be/UYVDnaQ8jZg) to bring the story to life.

We also built on feedback from the **GG22 Retrospective**, introducing improvements like **multi-lingual support** across Discord and Telegram, and expanding **On-Demand Bookings** to better meet the community's availability. These proved to be highly effective.

#### Network = Net Worth

Thanks to Mathilda, we hosted an [**Expert Session on COCM**](https://youtu.be/rfjs7GRaECQ) featuring Joel Miller.
We also organized a [**Karma GAP Workshop**](https://youtu.be/-xMLKmNxOxc) focused on **impact tracking and milestone reporting**—critical components for accountability within the ecosystem.

### Donny Lewis - Application Support, Marketing, Markdown, QF Explainers

**Experience**

The experience was wonderful. Let's Grow DAO had a well mapped out scope of work to execute with tasks suited to each members strengths. This allowed for a very efficient way to do what I love to do and that is help others, find ways to help themselves. Speaking to potential grantees, past grantees that were having issues and the broader community about the Gitcoin Grants ecosystem is always a pleasure.

**Value Add of Participation**

The community lead aspect of this I think is a huge value add for the overall optics of the Gitcoin ecosystem. Gitcoin has always been about community and funding what matters. A community lead onboarding and education program truly supports that ethos. The ability to have multiple options to receive support for potential grantees from office hours to 1:1 video calls that could be scheduled at the grantees convenience to go along with workshops, and informational spaces, videos and an all in one landing page focused purely on known FAQ's is a big help. I personally had quite a number of grantees reach out to me after only a dm where I shared the available resources to say, thank you. When dealing with people it is always best to meet them where they are, some need a 1:1 meeting, some just need a quick question answered, others just need to know where to look. I think this program did a good job of providing this layered approach.

**Opportunities for Improvement**

As discussed, the discord proved challenging for some grantees to navigate and without an official badge or notification of who were are, new people could be reluctant to take advice from "some random pfp".

Time would be beneficial for future programs. Having the selection of the RFP earlier allows for a focused use of all resources. It is not fair to expect that people will work on something that has not yet been secured. A bit more time would allow for more curated materials and promotion. It is said people need to see something seven times before acting on it. Time to make sure the program managers can get things seen by as many people as possible, and hitting that seven times threshold would be great.

**Free Flow**

Just from a personal perspective. I would like to say thank you for the opportunity to help in this capacity. I was extremely impressed by the video's produced, especially those by Karla and Syntropic's dreamers journey animation. Thank you to Wasabi, for the careful organization that helped empower me to do what I love most and lend a helping hand where I be able. Lastly, thanks again to the Gitcoin team for allowing us to have this opportunity to be part of the mission of Funding What Matters.

### Jimi Cohen - Reviewing Applications, Mastering Grants Stack

**Experience**

It was great having 1:1s with the curious grantees & dropping alpha on spaces to help grantees market themselves. Had a challenge in Discord getting the Citizen role. Maybe I had to get that when I joined ages ago. Would love to be in there helping more.

**Value Add of Participation**

Having 24/7 grantee support is massive value, especially from experienced grantees. The many 1:1s really enables us to go deep with the grantees to help them with the daunting process of applying & spreading the word.

**Opportunities for Improvement**

Due to a foreseen potential conflict of interest, Let's GROW DAO did not publicly promote Let's GROW Live during this RFP. Being able to promote publicly enables grantees to discover the opportunity of hosting, even if their projects aren't publicly available for us to DM yet. So some clarity of whether we're allowed to promote LGL during the RFP as one of the ways for ALL grantees to get exposure & promote themselves would further open up the door of opportunity for grantees to get more exposure during GG rounds.

**Free Flow**

Had some struggles web scraping GitHib & doing outreach to many builders. Nailing this would open up a lot of doors. Also onboarding open source builders from other ecosystems would be great.
